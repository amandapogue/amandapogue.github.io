what is ex philosophorum?

the brooklyn museum hosted an exhibit entitled copy machine manifestos: artists who make zines in 2024; both that exhibit and a reflection on past lives were the inspiration for this project.

  • looking for a monologue?

wait, what is a zine?

  • what?

    zines are typically self-produced, limited-batch print work. often authored and produced by a single person or a small collective. by nature they tend to be very DIY, using easily accessible technology, that can easily mass produce materials, such as photocopiers.

  • quoi?

    there are many kinds of ziness: art, poetry, political, fanzines, comics, DIY (there are definitely meta-zines about making zines, but also zines about making other stuff), literary (think: essays and short stories), personal, fan zines, and many more. this zine is intended to be a collaborative, correspondence zine - typically collective zines all focus on a singular topic at a time (like a special edition journal) but that's not the point of this zine.

  • ¿qué?

    for all of its issues grad school brought together a bunch of incredibly stubborn, incredibly interesting folks hell-bent on some obscure topic that literally a handful of people would fight for from atop soapboxes (or from the shoulders of (sic) giants, if some people's dedications are to be believed). each of those people were also, at times, super obsessed with other topics (qualified or not) that they unleashed on us all over shitty beers, even shitty sweet wine, camp fires, professor whiskey, DIY'd pizzas, and foods labelled "tastier than they look". i miss it. and if you do too, you're gonna like the rest of this page.
