Sample Uses of Qualtrics

A few samples of how we've used Qualtrics in the past in our lab, and some sample surveys to download and look at.

Simple Survey

For the past two years we've been collecting peer review scores for final project presentations in our class (Language Development). The students are asked to rate their peers on three categories: Research Question, Method, and Results. The reviews are anonymous and the students can provide comments for the presenters, to give them some feedback on their projects that could be useful for their final write-up.

Since this is a very simple task, the design of this survey is very bare bones. The students will get to select the group that is currently presenting (their names have been replaced in this sample version with the names of members of our lab), will get three sliders to rate their peers, and a comment box. When they are finished grading one group they can submit their reviews, and then refresh the page to submit new reviews of the next group.

Complex Experimental Design

Earlier in 2016 we published a paper ( Pogue, Kurumada, & Tanenhaus, 2016) in our lab looking at how people will update their expectations for the kinds of referring expressions a speaker might make, given one's prior expectations for how people might typically refer, and the evidence one has with that particular speaker. We ran these studies online using Qualtrics and Mechanical Turk. The following sample is what we presented to participants in Experiment 2B of this paper. This study has two phases: an exposure phase (20 trials), and a generalization phase (24 trials). Participants are randomly assigned speaker conditions, and test list conditions. The order of trial presentation is fully randomized. The participant has to consent to participate, and will listen to instructions about the task. We have a catch question to ensure they fully listened to the instructions, and they are required to answer on each trial to continue.

Some of the features to note in this design:
Things set up specifically for Mechanical Turk: for example, we ask people to submit a code at the completion of the task to the HIT page on MTurk. This survey generates a random 6-digit code for each survey taker, which it gives to them at the end of the task (we could later look at the output from MTurk and the output to Qualtrics to match participants, but really this is just done to ensure that the people getting paid actually did the study).
Setting Up Condition Randomization: Qualtrics allows us to set up embedded data (essentially generate condition labels), and randomly assign survey takers to these conditions. This is actually incredibly helpful when looking at the output.
Randomization of Trial Order: Qualtrics allow you to fully or partially randomize your trials (so you can fix the order of some questions, and randomly present other questions within that order, you can fully randomize the trials - as we have in two different ways: within a block, or as seperate blocks).
Controls: Participants cannot leave the audio instruction trial page until enough time has elapsed that they should have listened to all of the instructions. They have to provide a keyword from the end of the instructions to continue, they must provide answers on each trial to move one (exactly one response can be given).

In later tasks we used even more features of Qualtrics (e.g., display features, such as displaying a follow up question on the same page if someone answers "Yes" to an earlier question). But as a starter there are a lot of complicated features in the design of this task.
